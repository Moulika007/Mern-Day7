What is Robotics?

Robotics is an interdisciplinary field combining computer science, engineering, and design to create autonomous or semi-autonomous machines. Robots are designed to mimic, replace, or assist humans in tasks across industries like manufacturing, healthcare, agriculture, logistics, and defence.

How Robotics Evolved

  • Industrial Revolution: Focus on engineering and science.
  • 1930s: NC machines introduced.
  • 1937: Griffith Taylor built the first industrial robot.
  • 1954: George Devol patented the first industrial robot.
  • 1990s: Remote-controlled robots synchronized industry operations.

Isaac Asimov’s Three Laws of Robotics shaped ethical thinking in robotics.

Author: Moulika

Applications of Robotics

  • Manufacturing: Welding, painting, assembly lines.
  • Healthcare: Surgery assistance, rehabilitation exoskeletons.
  • Logistics: Warehouse inventory, sorting, packaging.
  • Agriculture: Drones for planting and harvesting.
  • Education: Teaching STEM and languages.
  • Space Exploration: Rovers like NASA’s Curiosity.
  • Defence: Bomb disposal robots, surveillance drones.
  • Smart Cities: Navigation, security patrols.
